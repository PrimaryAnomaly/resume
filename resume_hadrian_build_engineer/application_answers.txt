Why are you applying? What excites you the most?

At Hanwha Systems I helped design the architecture for a high-throughput satellite manufacturing facility while also doing hands-on hardware builds and integration on the factory floor. That experience of working both sides, thinking about production workflows and system layouts while actually building and troubleshooting the hardware, is what got me hooked on manufacturing systems engineering. Hadrian is doing this at a scale and speed that nobody else is attempting. Building autonomous factories for aerospace and defense from the ground up, not just optimizing existing lines, is exactly the kind of problem I want to work on. The Special Projects team especially excites me because it's first-generation systems, rapid iteration, and real hardware. That's where I do my best work.

What's the most relevant project you've worked on that makes you a great fit for this role?

At Hanwha Systems I led hardware build execution for military SAR satellite programs from subsystem integration through system-level acceptance. I designed the Electrical Ground Support Equipment and automated work cell configurations that the assembly team used daily, wrote the build procedures and acceptance criteria, and worked directly with design engineers to flag manufacturability issues early so we could fix them before they became production problems. When equipment or system anomalies came up during integration I led the troubleshooting and drove fixes to keep the line moving. On top of that I contributed to the architecture design for a new high-throughput satellite manufacturing facility, defining production workflows and system layouts from scratch. That combination of hands-on build execution, DFM feedback loops with design teams, and manufacturing facility planning is a direct match for what this role needs.
